2.1 BITCOIN

The first and most well-known cryptocurrency and blockchain platform enables peer-to-peer digital transactions without the need for intermediaries.Bitcoin operates on a decentralized network, meaning that there is no central authority or intermediary controlling its transactions. This is achieved through the use of a peer-to-peer network, where every participant in the network has an equal say in the verification and recording of transactions.[For money transaction]

2.2 ETHEREUM

A decentralized platform that enables the development of smart contracts and decentralized applications (dApps) using its native cryptocurrency, Ether (ETH).[For money transaction, dApps based on this technology ] Ethereum's most notable feature is its support for smart contracts, which are self-executing contracts with the terms of the agreement between buyer and seller being directly written into lines of code. This allows for decentralized applications (DApps) to be built on top of the Ethereum blockchain.Ethereum has faced challenges with scalability, as the network can become congested during times of high usage. However, several scalability solutions are being developed, including sharding and layer-two solutions like Plasma and Rollups, to increase the network's capacity and reduce transaction costs.

2.3 RIPPLE

A real-time gross settlement system currency exchange, and remittance network that utilizes its native cryptocurrency, XRP, for fast and low-cost international transactions.[Digital Currency Exchange] Ripple is designed to be a fast and efficient payment protocol, with transactions settling in seconds. This is achieved through the use of a consensus mechanism that does not require proof-of-work mining, which can be slow and energy-intensive. Ripple Labs has worked closely with financial regulators and institutions to ensure that its technology meets regulatory requirements and can be integrated into existing financial systems.Overall, Ripple's focus on fast, efficient, and compliant cross-border payments has made it a popular choice among financial institutions and individuals looking for a secure and reliable payment solution.

2.4 HYPERLEDGER FARIC

A permissioned blockchain platform developed by the Linux Foundation's Hyperledger project, designed for enterprise use cases and tailored for specific business requirements.[Creating Blockchain] Hyperledger Fabric is designed with a modular architecture, meaning that different components can be customized and replaced as needed. This allows organizations to tailor the platform to their specific needs, and also makes it more flexible for future upgrades and enhancements.

2.5 CORDA

An open-source blockchain platform developed by R3, designed for enterprise applications, particularly in the financial sector, to facilitate secure and private transactions between businesses.[Transaction - Private] Corda's architecture is designed to ensure privacy and confidentiality by ensuring that sensitive data is only shared with the parties that need it. This is achieved through the use of a "point-to-point" messaging system that allows parties to communicate directly with each other without broadcasting their transactions to the entire network.Corda enables developers to write and execute complex smart contracts using a simple and easy-to-use programming language called Kotlin. These smart contracts are designed to automate business processes and eliminate the need for intermediaries.

2.6 EOS

A blockchain platform designed for high-performance decentralized applications, offering scalability, flexibility, and governance mechanisms through its delegated proof-of-stake (DPoS) consensus algorithm.[Transaction based on PoS, we will see this in "How blockchain Works" module] EOS uses a combination of consensus mechanisms, including DPoS and Byzantine fault tolerance (BFT), to ensure the security and integrity of the network. Additionally, smart contracts on EOS are sandboxed, which means that they run in a secure and isolated environment.EOS is designed to be interoperable with other blockchain platforms, which means that it can communicate and exchange data with other decentralized networks.

2.7 STELLAR

A decentralized platform that enables fast and low-cost cross-border transactions and token issuance, with a focus on financial inclusion and serving the unbanked population. Digital transaction with low cost transaction fee, we will see more about this in "How blockchain Works" module. Stellar is designed to process transactions quickly and efficiently, with an average transaction time of just 2-5 seconds. This is achieved through the use of a unique consensus algorithm known as the Stellar Consensus Protocol (SCP).Stellar is a highly efficient and scalable blockchain platform that is well-suited for facilitating fast and cost-effective cross-border transactions. It offers a range of features that make it a popular choice for businesses, developers, and individuals who require a secure and efficient payment system.

2.8 TRON

A blockchain platform that aims to create a decentralized entertainment ecosystem, allowing content creators to directly connect with consumers without intermediaries.Just like Youtube but in decentralized manner, which helps creator to get money without need of any intermediators like youtube. Tron is designed to process transactions quickly and efficiently, with the capacity to handle up to 2,000 transactions per second (TPS).Tron enables developers to write and deploy smart contracts using the Solidity programming language, which is widely used in the Ethereum ecosystem. This makes it easier for developers to create dApps that meet specific business requirements.

2.9 VE-CHAIN

A blockchain platform that focuses on supply chain management and product authenticity, providing traceability and transparency for businesses and consumers. VeChain is designed to enable businesses to track and manage their supply chains in a more transparent and efficient manner. The platform uses radio-frequency identification (RFID) and near-field communication (NFC) technology to track products from the point of origin to the point of consumption.VeChain is designed to be highly scalable, which means that it can handle a large number of transactions without compromising on performance. It uses a Proof-of-Authority (PoA) consensus mechanism that allows for faster transaction processing times compared to other blockchain platforms. VeChain has a unique governance model that allows token holders to vote for Authority Masternodes who are responsible for validating transactions and making decisions about the future development of the platform.

2.10 IOTA

A distributed ledger technologydesigned for the Internet of Things (IoT) ecosystem, utilizing a unique directed acyclic graph (DAG) structure called the Tangle for feeless microtransactions and data integrity. In simple terms, parallel processing means that multiple transactions can be handled at the same time instead of one after another. It's like having multiple lanes on a highway, allowing more cars to move simultaneously, which speeds up the overall flow of traffic. In the case of IOTA's Tangle, the transactions are organized in a web-like structure instead of a straight line. This structure allows many transactions to be processed concurrently by different computers in the network. It's like having multiple workers working on different tasks simultaneously, which makes things faster.
