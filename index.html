1. WEB 3.0 :

In a traditional app or website, there's a central authority that controls everything - they make the rules and make decisions about how things should work. In a web3 app, there's something called a DAO instead. A DAO is like a group of people who all have a say in how things work, instead of just one person or organization. Instead of having one person or organization making all the decisions, a DAO is controlled by people who own special tokens. These tokens give them the right to vote on decisions about how the app should work, and they can even propose changes themselves.

You can get these tokens by participating in the app - for example, by providing computing power or helping to verify transactions. You can also buy them on an exchange. Once you have the tokens, you have a say in how the app works and what decisions are made. So, in a web3 app, instead of having a single person or company in charge, the users themselves get to make the decisions. 3. Healthcare :

Blockchain can improve the security and privacy of healthcare records by creating a decentralized and immutable ledger for storing patient data. It can enable interoperability between different healthcare providers, enhance data accuracy, and facilitate secure sharing of medical information.

4. dApps!! :

A decentralized application(dApp) is an application that runs on a peer-to-peer network of computers or nodes, utilizing smart contracts and blockchain technology for its operations. Unlike traditional applications that rely on centralized servers, dApps are distributed across the network, making them more resilient and resistant to censorship.

5. Decentralized Finance (DeFi):

Blockchain enables the development of decentralized financial applications, offering services such as lending, borrowing, trading, and asset management without the need for traditional intermediaries. Smart contracts play a crucial role in automating financial transactions and reducing reliance on centralized authorities.
