2.1 PUBLIC BLOCK CHAIN

There are various types of block chain among all it is broadly classified into PRIVATE and PUBLIC block chain.

A public blockchain is an open and decentralized network where anyone can join, participate, and validate transactions.

Examples include Bitcoin and Ethereum. Public blockchains are maintained by a distributed network of nodes, and their data is transparent and accessible to anyone. Any one can join the network and leave as they wish. The first cryptocurrency Bitcoin uses the public chain which is also a reason for its recognition. but they may also face challenges like scalability and transaction speed due to the need for consensus among a large number of participants

Consensus mechanism which is the process of validating and verifying transactions so that it can be broadcast to the network and gains valid transaction.

The security of a public blockchain primarily based on the consensus mechanism, which ensures that the majority of participants agree on the validity of transactions and the order in which they are added to the blockchain. This consensus are the agreement or protocol that agreed by the participants in the chain. The consensus mechanism can be based on proof of work (PoW), proof of stake (PoS), or other algorithms [ we covered this topics under Technical Terms section ]. As far now please consider that bitcoin uses PoW ,ethereum uses PoS and these algorithms are used to solve some complex problems as a result they get HASH value.

2.2 PRIVATE BLOCK CHAIN

Private blockchains, also known as permissioned or enterprise blockchains, are blockchain networks that restrict access and participation to a specific group of entities.



Characteristics and advantages of private blockchains:

1. Permissioned Access: These blockchains are typically used within organizations. This allows organizations to maintain control over who can participate, ensuring that only trusted person are involved in the network.

2. Enhanced Privacy: Private blockchains offer greater privacy and confidentiality compared to public blockchains. Transaction details and data are shared only among the approved participants.

3. Higher Scalability: Since private blockchains operate within a restricted network, they typically exhibit better scalability compared to public blockchains. With fewer participants and transactions to process, private blockchains can achieve higher transaction and faster validation times.

4. Cost Efficiency: Private blockchains can offer cost advantages in terms of transaction fees and energy consumption compared to public blockchains. Since private blockchains do not rely on mining and the associated energy-intensive processes, they can be more energy-efficient and have lower transaction costs. In simple terms, it does not use PoW which requires high energy instead they use any other low energy consuming algorithms since only small quantity of people is there as compare to Public blockchain.

Few common consensus mechanism used in private blockchains:

1. Proof of Stake (PoS)

2. Practical Byzantine Fault Tolerance (PBFT)

3. Delegated Proof of Stake (DPoS)

4. Federated Consensus

5. Practical Byzantine Fault Tolerance (PBFT)













2.3 OTHER TYPES OF BLOCKCHAIN

A consortium blockchain is a hybrid between public and private blockchains. It is operated by a group of organizations or companies instead of a single entity

Hybrid Blockchain: A hybrid blockchain combines elements of both public and private blockchains. It allows for both public and private transactions, providing flexibility and customization based on specific use cases.



Sidechain:A sidechain is an independent blockchain that operates alongside the main blockchain, also known as the parent chain or main chain. It allows for the transfer of assets or data between the two chains, enabling certain functionalities or features that may not be available or feasible on the main blockchain alone
