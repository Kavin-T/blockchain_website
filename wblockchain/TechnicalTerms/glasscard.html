1.Decentralization

It is main term related to blockchain technology we should keep in mind. It refers to the distribution of authority, control, and decision-making across a network of nodes or participants rather than being concentrated in a single central authority or entity. It is a fundamental principle of blockchain systems. Let's explain decentralization in the context of blockchain with a story example: Once upon a time, there was a town called Blockchainville. In Blockchainville, there was a central marketplace controlled by a single authority, Mayor Centralizer. All transactions and trades in the town had to go through Mayor Centralizer, who decided the rules, validated transactions, and had the power to change prices or reject trades at will. One day, the people of Blockchainville became dissatisfied with the central authority's control .They decided to create a new marketplace based on blockchain technology. They formed a network of computers called "nodes" and connected them together. In this new decentralized marketplace, there was no single central authority like Mayor Centralizer. Instead, all the nodes in the network had equal rights and responsibilities. Each node had a copy of the entire marketplace's records, known as the blockchain. Whenever someone wanted to make a transaction or trade, they would broadcast their request to all the nodes in the network. The nodes would then verify the transaction using a consensus mechanism, where they collectively agreed on the validity of the transaction and its place in the order of transactions.

2.Participants

Participants in a blockchain refer to the entities or individuals that are part of the blockchain network and actively engage in its operations. These participants play different roles and contribute to the functioning of the blockchain ecosystem. In a broad terms Miners,codewriter[Smart Contract] , Sender,Receivers play different roles and contribute to the functioning of the blockchain ecosystem.

3.Node

Nodes are computers or devices that participate in maintaining the blockchain network. They validate transactions, store a copy of the blockchain, and communicate with other nodes to reach consensus on the state of the blockchain.

4.Private Key

A private key is associated with a specific user or participant. It is 256-bit random number is generated, and designated as the private key. Kept secure and locked using a passphrase. To generate random number algorithms like SHA-256 used.

5.Public key

It is a cryptographic key that is publicly shared and used for various security operations, such as encryption, verification, and establishing secure communication channels. ECC algorithm is applied to the private key, to get a unique public key. This is the private public key pair.

6.Cryptography

Cryptography is a method of protecting information and communications through the use of codes, so that only those for whom the information is intended can read and process it.
